WADI RUM VILLAGE -- TRAVEL IN THE FUTURE

CHAOMING & JUNHONG
(2019)

“In a landscape where nothing officially exists (otherwise it would not be “desert”) absolutely anything becomes thinkable and may consequently happen”. – Reyner Banham (“Scenes in America Deserta”)

 

Wadi Rum Village: Travel in The Future (VR tech) from Ming L on Vimeo.

Wadi Rum village, situated in southern Jordan, is featured with a varied desert landscape consisting of a range of narrow gorges, natural arches, towering cliffs, ramps, massive landslides and caverns, and a cultural history of human’s more than 12000 years’ occupation and interaction with nature. With the development of tourism, Wadi Rum is getting increasingly popular and the economy has developed accordingly. However, some other things are getting deprived. Tourism presents the greatest current threat with poorly regulated off road driving by tour operators, construction of illegal campsites, and self-guided tourists causing vegetation damage and also threatening the integrity of the site. The red sand, one of the unique features of this place, is being destroyed by the wheel tracks. Also, the illegal hunting in Wadi Rum have bad effects on biodiversity: the Arabian oryx was hunted to extinction 80 years ago. There is no regulation on limiting the number of visitors or a way to distributing them made yet.

In confronting the threats and our goal to protect our natural resource and living environment, the big idea is to propose a new one-way rail to guide the tourist around the village in the near future, adding to the current division of intensive use area for tourist camping and driving and wild zones for hikers and camel riders. In this case, people can not only get the view of the landscape remotely, but also can get off in many points to perceive the living atmosphere and get knowledge of what the genuine desert life is like. It is aimed to protect local red sand from four-wheel vehicles’ damage and adapt to the result of coming climate change.

  • Field guide use

    For future travelers to Wadi Rum Village, they can use the stripe to learn the condition and how it will change in the future. They will learn by scanning the whole thing to see an overview, and wear it to the field. They can scan the five segments at any time, to see how mountain, wind, water, human and nonhuman species are changing. There will also be signs in featured spots to guide people to do so.

    Mobius stripe is a one-sided thing, representing the possible future under the climate change and the control of people, it is going to be something similar to ancient times, which conceives an idea of coexistence of ancient and future. Also, the proposed trail is a one-sided one, tourists are to pass by a spot twice and have different feelings and experiences each time.
